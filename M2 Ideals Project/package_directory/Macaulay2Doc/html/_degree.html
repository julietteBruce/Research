Macaulay2Doc :: degree

degree

Description

Degree is a common name, meaning different things for different kinds of mathematical objects. In Macaulay2, there are currently three related, yet different notions of degree:

Degree of polynomials or vectors of such

  • degree(RingElement)
  • degree(Vector)
  • degree(RingElement,RingElement) -- degree with respect to a variable
  • degree(ProjectiveHilbertPolynomial)

Degree of ideals, varieties and modules

  • degree(Ideal)
  • degree(Ring)
  • degree(Module)
  • degree(ProjectiveVariety)

Degree of homomorphisms

  • degree(Matrix) (missing documentation)
  • degree(ChainComplexMap) (missing documentation)
  • degree(GradedModuleMap) (missing documentation)

See also

  • degreeLength -- the length of the degree vector
  • degreesRing -- the ring or monoid of degrees

  • degree(Matrix) (missing documentation)

  • degree(ProjectiveHilbertPolynomial)
  • degree(ProjectiveVariety)
  • degree(Ring)
  • degree(RingElement)
  • "degree(Vector)" -- see degree(RingElement)
  • degree(RingElement,RingElement) -- degree with respect to a variable
